Accessibility

how we tested the code for accessibility .

Upload the site to hosting

After we completed the initial design process for the project, we uploaded it to a hosting that allows us to access the site via the domain and our project In order to make it easier to deal with the project in terms of conducting tests on it by means of WAVE Web Accessibility Evaluation Tools

After that, we entered the site link in the test field of the tool


The test result

The test tool showed us many errors in the design, and the errors fall into five main categories, and most of the errors were errors related to some empty and repeated links and alternative texts for some images, and among the most frequent errors on the site was the low contrast between the color of the white text and the degree of bright green which we chose in the beginning





The solutions we implemented

We tried to improve the site as much as possible to suit the universal design. Among the problems that we explored are the problems associated with low contrast, where we changed the degree of green to a darker degree than before. We also got rid of repetitive and empty links and errors by skipping the heading level. In addition to some changes that the tool could not warn us about, such as some text sizes and shadows

The result we got after optimization

After re-uploading the site again and using the same tool, we were able to get a better result than the previous one, as we took into account the universal design and accessibility as much as possible through a better arrangement of titles according to their importance and the use of highly concentrated colors that are in line with people with visual disabilities greatly..
